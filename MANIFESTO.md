# Manifesto

Eye of Horus exists to explore a simple question with serious discipline:

What if code were geometry first?

The project values curiosity over hype, rigor over mystique, and public critique over private certainty. It treats geometry as a design lens for computation, not as proof of superiority.

We will not claim production readiness before it exists. We will not publish benchmark narratives without reproducible evidence. We will not convert metaphors into scientific claims.

We will build carefully, document honestly, and invite the programming-language community to test the idea in the open.
